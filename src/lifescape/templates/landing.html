Working rule

What cannot work should never be averaged away.

01 / The difference

A shortlist is only useful when you can see why it exists.

Most comparison tools collapse everything into one score. Lifescape separates the decision into hard gates, ranked preferences, source quality, and sensitivity—so a town cannot compensate for failing something essential.

02 / Follow the route

From “maybe there” to a decision you can inspect.

The path stays legible from the first constraint to the final report. Nothing important disappears behind a composite score.

See the finished decision →
  1. 01
    Frame

    Set the edges of the map.

    Define the purchase ceiling, planning age, household, and the towns worth comparing. These inputs remain visible and editable.

  2. 02
    Inspect

    Read the evidence before the score.

    Review completeness before scoring. Blank critical fields remain unknown; Lifescape does not invent a value to keep a candidate alive.

  3. 03
    Decide

    Keep the survivors and rejections together.

    See the eligible ranking, criteria, stability across 1,000 simulations, and every town that failed or could not clear a gate.
